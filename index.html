A Roadmap for Career & College Advising in Every Kentucky School

The Postsecondary Advising Scope & Sequence gives educators a clear, grade-by-grade framework for guiding students from career exploration to postsecondary action — starting in middle school.

Why a Scope & Sequence?

Kentucky students deserve consistent, high-quality advising no matter where they go to school. But too often, career and college planning happens in isolated moments — a single college fair, a rushed scheduling session, or a senior-year scramble for financial aid.

The Postsecondary Advising Scope & Sequence changes that. Developed by the Kentucky Advising Academy in partnership with the Council on Postsecondary Education, it provides a structured progression of advising experiences so students build knowledge and confidence year after year.

It’s not a curriculum. It’s a flexible framework that meets educators where they are — whether you’re a school counselor, a classroom teacher, a CTE instructor, or an administrator setting the vision for your school’s advising culture.

Four Focus Areas Across Every Grade

Each grade level addresses all four focus areas, ensuring students develop well-rounded knowledge and skills throughout their journey:

💼
Career Exploration
Interest surveys, career clusters, occupations research, and connecting interests to career paths
🎓
Postsecondary Opportunities
Degree types, postsecondary pathways, early college options, and application processes
💰
Financial Literacy
Budgeting, saving, credit, scholarships, FAFSA, and connecting education to financial outcomes
🤝
Essential Skills
Social capital, goal-setting, networking, leadership, and professional communication

How It Works

The Scope & Sequence spans grades 6 through 12 and is organized into two guides — one for middle school and one for high school. Each guide includes:

Grade-level outcomes — What students should understand and experience by the end of each year
Guiding questions — To frame advising conversations with students
Learning experiences — With linked lesson plans ready for the classroom or advisory period
Evidence of learning — So educators can see whether students are on track
ILP & Student Game Plan connections — Tied directly to the Individual Learning Plan and Student Game Plan Template
A suggested calendar — Monthly themes (middle school) to help schools plan when to deliver advising activities throughout the year

Each lesson covers about 60 minutes of content and can be delivered during a class period, broken into chunks for advisory, or extended with additional activities. Ideally, students experience one lesson to revisit their career interest survey and one lesson for each of the four focus areas — five total per year.

Built for a Whole-School Approach

The Scope & Sequence is designed so that advising isn’t the job of one person — it’s a shared responsibility across the school.

🏫 Administrators
Set the advising culture, build time into the master schedule, and communicate expectations for school-wide advising
📚 School Counselors
Guide advising processes, push into classrooms, coordinate with families, and support informed course selection
📝 Classroom Teachers
Connect subject matter to real-world careers and integrate essential skills like goal-setting into coursework
🛠 CTE Instructors
Leverage student organizations (like DECA, FBLA, SkillsUSA), work-based learning, and industry experiences to deepen career exploration
